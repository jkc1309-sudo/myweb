How should AI adapt to people?

Project 1

Adaptive Museum and Narrative Systems

CuriNarrator

Curiosity-driven AI narratives for museum exploration

An AI-driven storytelling system that stimulates visitor curiosity through uncertainty, information gain, and progress feedback strategies while keeping narratives grounded and verifiable.

  • Problem: Museum interpretation often struggles to sustain curiosity and personal relevance.
  • System: LLM-based dialogue generation plus retrieval-augmented narrative support.
  • Method: System design, narrative strategy modeling, and user-centered evaluation.

Adaptive Digital Human Museum Guide

Embodied guide agents shaped by user state and scene context

A framework and prototype for museum digital humans that adapt image, role setting, narrative tone, and interaction strategy to create more responsive cultural experiences.

  • Problem: Fixed-script guides cannot respond to visitor curiosity, pace, or engagement signals.
  • System: A layered adaptive framework covering foundational setup, design choices, and interaction strategy.
  • Focus: Observable user states, narrative adaptation, and scene-semantic matching.

Project 2

AI Agents for Learning

SimTeaching

An AI-powered virtual classroom for pre-service teacher training

A virtual classroom system where GPT-driven student agents in a 3D environment help pre-service teachers practice classroom interaction with more realism and repeatability.

  • Problem: Teacher training often lacks realistic, repeatable interaction rehearsal.
  • System: Unity-based classroom, virtual students, speech interaction, and a “GPT Mask” prompt framework.
  • Outcome: Published in IJHCI and shown to improve teacher self-efficacy compared with traditional rehearsal.

Project 3

Tangible Interaction, Child-Computer Interaction, and Design

See, Hear, Touch, Smell, and, ...Eat!

A multi-sensory puzzle game for food literacy

A tangible, child-centered interactive game that combines multiple senses to support food education and healthier eating behavior.

  • Focus: Tangible interaction, food literacy, and child-computer interaction.
  • Recognition: ACM IDC 2024 Best Short Paper Award.
  • Contribution: Shows how multi-sensory play can support learning and behavior change.
